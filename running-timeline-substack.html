776 BCE

Ancient Olympics
The Stadion — First Olympic Race
▾ expand
The ancient Olympic Games kick off at Olympia with a single event: the stade, a roughly 192-meter sprint down the length of the stadium. Coroebus of Elis, a cook, becomes the first recorded Olympic champion in history.
724 BCE
🏃
Ancient Olympics
The Diaulos — Two-Length Race Added
▾ expand
A two-length race (roughly equivalent to today's 400m) is introduced to the Olympic program, doubling the distance and the challenge for competitors.
720 BCE
🌿
Ancient Olympics
The Dolichos — First Long Distance Race
▾ expand
A long-distance foot race comparable to the modern 1,500–5,000m events is added to the Olympics. Akanthos of Sparta wins the inaugural edition. Distance running is now an Olympic discipline.
520 BCE
⚔️
Ancient Olympics
Race in Armor — Hoplitodromos
▾ expand
Soldiers compete in full military gear — helmet, shield, and greaves — testing both athletic and martial readiness. Running is officially part of warfare preparation.
393 CE
🕯️
Ancient Olympics
Ancient Olympics Abolished
▾ expand
Roman Emperor Theodosius I ends all pagan games in Greece, closing over 1,000 years of ancient Olympic competition. Running as an organized sport goes dark for nearly 1,500 years.
1819
🦊
Pre-Modern
First Organized Cross Country Race
▾ expand
The first organized cross-country competition — a "hare and hounds" paperchase — is held at Shrewsbury School in England, planting the seeds of cross country as a formal discipline.
1896
🔥
Modern Olympics
First Modern Olympics — Athens
▾ expand
The modern Olympic Games are revived in Athens. Running events include the 100m, 400m, 800m, 1500m, 110m hurdles, and the first-ever modern marathon — won by Greek hero Spiridon Louis in front of a roaring home crowd.
1897
🏙️
Milestones
Boston Marathon Founded
▾ expand
Inspired directly by the 1896 Olympic marathon, the Boston Marathon is held for the first time — becoming the world's oldest annual marathon and the gold standard of road racing prestige.
1912
🌍
Modern Olympics
IAAF Founded
▾ expand
The International Association of Athletics Federations is established, creating a global governing body for track and field and officially standardizing world records for the first time.
1920
💧
Modern Olympics
Steeplechase Debuts at Olympics
▾ expand
The 3,000m steeplechase makes its Olympic debut at the Antwerp Games, adding water jumps and barriers to the distance running program.
1928
♀️
Modern Olympics
Women's Track Debuts — Amsterdam
▾ expand
Women compete in Olympic running events for the first time at the Amsterdam Games — running the 100m, 4×100m relay, and 800m. A long-overdue beginning.
1952
⚡
Modern Olympics
Zátopek's Triple Gold — Helsinki
▾ expand
Czech runner Emil Zátopek completes the most remarkable feat in Olympic distance running history: winning gold in the 5,000m, 10,000m, and marathon — all in the same Games. A record that may never be broken.
1954
⏱️
Milestones
Roger Bannister Breaks the 4-Minute Mile
▾ expand
On May 6 in Oxford, Roger Bannister runs the mile in 3:59.4 — becoming the first human to crack what was considered a physiological impossibility. Within 46 days, another runner breaks it too.
1960
🌍
Modern Olympics
Abebe Bikila — Barefoot Gold in Rome
▾ expand
Ethiopian Abebe Bikila wins the Rome Olympic marathon running entirely barefoot, setting a world record. He becomes the first Black African Olympic champion in athletics.
1967
✊
Milestones
Kathrine Switzer Runs Boston
▾ expand
Kathrine Switzer becomes the first woman to officially enter and run the Boston Marathon — registered as K.V. Switzer — despite a race official's attempt to physically remove her mid-race.
1970
🗽
Milestones
First New York City Marathon
▾ expand
The New York City Marathon launches with 127 runners looping Central Park. It grows into one of the world's largest and most celebrated road races — the spark of the modern running boom.
1972
🇺🇸
Modern Olympics
Frank Shorter Wins Olympic Marathon — Munich
▾ expand
Running in the city of his birth just days after the Munich Massacre, Frank Shorter surges at mile 11 and finishes more than 2 minutes ahead of the field — the first American to win the Olympic marathon gold in 64 years. His finish is briefly upstaged by a German impostor who enters the stadium ahead of him, but Shorter, knowing no one had passed him, never wavers. The win is widely credited with igniting the American running boom of the 1970s, inspiring a generation to lace up and hit the road.
1974
🇩🇪
Milestones
Berlin Marathon Founded
▾ expand
The Berlin Marathon is founded, running its first edition through the streets of West Berlin. It evolves into the world's fastest marathon course — hosting more world records than any other race, including Kipchoge's 2:01:09 and Kiptum's 2:00:35.
1977
🌬️
Milestones
Chicago Marathon Founded
▾ expand
The Chicago Marathon launches in its modern format with a flat, looped course through 29 neighborhoods. It becomes one of the fastest courses on earth — home to both the current men's (Kiptum, 2:00:35) and women's (Chepngetich, 2:09:56) world records.
1981
🎡
Milestones
London Marathon Founded
▾ expand
Olympic steeplechase champion Chris Brasher founds the London Marathon, inspired by running the NYC Marathon. The race passes Tower Bridge, the Tower of London, and Buckingham Palace — and becomes the world's largest annual charity fundraising event, with over 1.1 million applications for the 2026 edition.
1984
🏅
Modern Olympics
First Women's Olympic Marathon — Los Angeles
▾ expand
After an 88-year wait, women finally compete in the Olympic marathon. Joan Benoit Samuelson of the USA wins in dominant fashion. A milestone made possible by years of grassroots campaigning by athletes like Kathrine Switzer and Jacqueline Hansen.
2003
🇰🇪
Milestones
Paul Tergat Breaks 2:05 Marathon Barrier
▾ expand
Kenya's Paul Tergat runs 2:04:55 in Berlin — the first officially ratified marathon world record under modern rules, and the first ever sub-2:05 performance in history.
2006
⭐
Milestones
World Marathon Majors Series Launches
▾ expand
The Abbott World Marathon Majors (WMM) is officially founded on January 23, 2006, uniting the five most prestigious road races — Boston, London, Berlin, Chicago, and New York City — into a single points-based championship series. A $1 million prize purse elevates the elite competition to a new level.
2007
🗼
Milestones
Tokyo Marathon Founded
▾ expand
The Tokyo Marathon launches its inaugural edition, attracting nearly 36,000 participants through iconic landmarks including the Imperial Palace, Tokyo Skytree, and Ginza. It becomes the first Asian marathon to join the World Marathon Majors series, earning that status in 2013.
2025
🦘
Milestones
Sydney Marathon — The 7th Major
▾ expand
The TCS Sydney Marathon is inducted as the seventh Abbott World Marathon Major in November 2024, becoming the first Major in the Southern Hemisphere. Its point-to-point course runs past the Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge — the most scenic finish line in the series. The first edition as a Major runs on August 31, 2025.
2009
⚡
Milestones
Usain Bolt Runs 9.58 — Berlin
▾ expand
At the Berlin World Championships, Usain Bolt runs 9.58 seconds in the 100m — a world record that still stands today. He also holds the 200m world record at 19.19, set the same week.
2016
🥇
Modern Olympics
Kipchoge's First Olympic Gold — Rio
▾ expand
Eliud Kipchoge wins Olympic marathon gold in Rio de Janeiro, defeating the largest field in Olympic marathon history. A new era of distance dominance begins.
2018
🌐
Milestones
Kipchoge Sets World Record — 2:01:39
▾ expand
At the Berlin Marathon, Eliud Kipchoge runs 2:01:39 — slashing 78 seconds off the previous world record. Many consider it the greatest marathon performance in history.
2019
🚀
Milestones
Sub-2-Hour Marathon — Kipchoge in Vienna
▾ expand
On October 12, Eliud Kipchoge runs 1:59:40 in Vienna — the first human in recorded history to run a marathon in under two hours. The time doesn't count as an official world record due to pacing conditions, but it reshapes what anyone believes is possible. "No human is limited."
2023
💔
Milestones
Kelvin Kiptum — 2:00:35 in Chicago
▾ expand
Kelvin Kiptum shatters the marathon world record at the Chicago Marathon with a stunning 2:00:35 — the current official world record, and the closest anyone has come to sub-2 hours in competition. Tragically, Kiptum died in a car accident in early 2024.
2024
🗼
Modern Olympics
Paris Olympics — A New Generation
▾ expand
The Paris 2024 Games introduce new mixed relay formats. Kipchoge bows out of his final Olympic marathon at the 30km mark, closing a legendary chapter. A new generation of runners inherits the road.
